My name is Felippe, a control engineer from Brazil. 
Currently I hold a PhD position at TUK, Germany, working with reinforcement learning applied to self driving cars. My main goal is to develop an adaptive control system able to update the car behavior by using its experience (experience meaning data gathered while the car is running).

During my masters I developed a novel solution to solve a recurrent problem with airborne wind energy systems (more specifically with tethered airfoils): How to provide energy to the control unit that flies along with the airfoil? Connecting a cable to the ground may not be a good solution when you're flying at an altitude of 1000m (not only because of safety, but drag and weight are big problems as well). The developed system consists in a micro wind generator that uses the constant winds available at such high altitudes in order to charge the embedded battery.

For my bachelor thesis I developed a recommendation system for retail companies based on the customer demographic profile and its purchase history. The system, based in R language, combined classification methods (decision tree, K-nearest neighbors) with clustering (K-means). A genetic algorithm was used to find the optimal combination of products for a specific custumer.

A bit about my working experience: I have worked as a professional violinist, electronics technician, data scientist, and now I am a researcher. Who knows what comes next?
